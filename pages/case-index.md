<section id="by-theme" class="section section-alt">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Cases by Common Law Theme</h2>
    <p class="lead">
      Each case reveals timeless legal and moral principles. This organization shows
      which cases demonstrate each core revelation.
    </p>

    <h3>1. Public Trust Doctrine</h3>
    <p class="theme-description">
      Government agencies must act as fiduciaries, administering public resources for
      the people's benefit, not as profit-generating enterprises.
    </p>
    <ul class="case-list">
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/njta-v-barber/' | relative_url }}">NJTA v. Barber</a> — Toll authority as public trust</li>
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/atl-l-002794-25/' | relative_url }}">Barber v. Tumelty</a> — Attorney as fiduciary trustee</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>2. Proportionality in Commerce</h3>
    <p class="theme-description">
      Penalties and fees must be proportional to the offense and actual costs. Excessive
      fees constitute exploitation, not justice.
    </p>
    <ul class="case-list">
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/njta-v-barber/' | relative_url }}">NJTA v. Barber</a> — 24:1 fee-to-toll ratio challenges</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>3. Due Process & Conscience</h3>
    <p class="theme-description">
      Fair process is a sacred right requiring notice, opportunity to be heard, and
      decisions based on evidence and reason.
    </p>
    <ul class="case-list">
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/barber-nj-pcr-2024/' | relative_url }}">State v. Barber (PCR)</a> — Ineffective counsel deprives fair trial</li>
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/a-000308-25/' | relative_url }}">Barber v. State (A-000308-25)</a> — Procedural fairness in appeals</li>
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/a-000313-25/' | relative_url }}">Barber v. NJTA (A-000313-25)</a> — Notice and opportunity to challenge</li>
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/atl-24-001934/' | relative_url }}">State v. Barber (ATL-24-001934)</a> — Unlawful detention without cause</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>4. Fiduciary Duty & Good Faith</h3>
    <p class="theme-description">
      Those entrusted with power must exercise it in good faith, serving the interests
      of those they are sworn to protect.
    </p>
    <ul class="case-list">
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/atl-l-002794-25/' | relative_url }}">Barber v. Tumelty</a> — Attorney's duty to investigate and preserve defenses</li>
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/njta-v-barber/' | relative_url }}">NJTA v. Barber</a> — Public agency's duty to serve, not exploit</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>5. Stewardship Over Exploitation</h3>
    <p class="theme-description">
      Government exists to serve and steward, not to dominate and extract. Every exercise
      of power must be justified by service to the common good.
    </p>
    <ul class="case-list">
      <li><a href="{{ '/cases/njta-v-barber/' | relative_url }}">NJTA v. Barber</a> — Toll enforcement as stewardship vs. extraction</li>
      <li><strong>All PCR cases</strong> — Criminal justice as rehabilitation vs. punishment</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>
